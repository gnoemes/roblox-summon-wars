# Hooks & Retention v0.2

**Status:** Fixed baseline  
**Last updated:** 2026-03-08

---

## 1. Purpose

This document fixes the non-balance hooks that are critical for:
- first-session attraction;
- repeat engagement;
- short-term dopamine;
- social comparison;
- long-term retention.

This document is intentionally product-facing, not purely systemic.

---

## 2. Core Philosophy

A deep system is not enough.

The game needs:
- immediate clarity;
- frequent reward signals;
- visible growth;
- emotional spikes;
- social proof;
- recognizable fantasy;
- guided next steps.

### Core principle
The player should regularly feel:
- “something happened”
- “I got stronger”
- “I want one more fight”
- “I know what to do next”
- “I want that too”

---

## 3. High-Level Hook Pillars

1. **Anime-inspired presentation**
2. **Big visible numbers**
3. **Frequent dopamine moments**
4. **Social visibility**
5. **Dense reward cadence**
6. **Visible progression**
7. **Strong reveal moments**
8. **Prestige and comparison**
9. **Guided reveal of deeper systems**

---

## 4. Big Numbers and Visible Growth

The game should intentionally support:
- stronger DPS jumps;
- faster kills;
- big crits;
- dramatic region escalation;
- meaningful rarity spikes.

### Design rule
Growth should feel more like:
- 40 → 300 → 600 → 2,000 → 14,000
than:
- 15 → 18 → 22 → 30

---

## 5. Attack Speed as a Retention Stat

Attack Speed is not only a balance stat.
It is also a feel stat.

It affects:
- perceived power;
- visual intensity;
- farming satisfaction;
- the feeling that a fighter is “online”.

### Design direction
Higher-grade or more developed fighters should be allowed to:
- attack faster;
- chain effects more often;
- produce higher screen activity;
- feel dramatically stronger in motion.

---

## 6. Dense Reward Cadence

The game should provide rewards at multiple scales.

### Small rewards
Every 10–20 seconds:
- resources;
- XP;
- region progress;
- visible damage progress;
- kill streak value.

### Medium rewards
Every 1–3 minutes:
- new card;
- elite/chest spawn;
- level-up;
- clear milestone;
- node completion;
- guided chain progress.

### Large rewards
Every 5–15 minutes:
- rare card;
- boss win;
- unlock;
- region progress gate;
- major power spike.

---

## 7. Reveal Moments

Important reveal candidates:
- fighter card opening;
- Rare/Epic/Legendary pull;
- boss reward;
- milestone completion;
- new region unlock;
- first useful Recommended action after friction.

The game should not treat good rewards like silent inventory updates.

---

## 8. Rare Encounter Spikes

Rare field replacements remain a key hook.

After a normal target dies, the next spawn may become:
- a chest;
- an elite;
- a rare enemy.

### Why this matters
It breaks monotony and creates:
- surprise;
- excitement;
- mini-jackpot moments;
- “one more fight” energy.

---

## 9. Social Visibility

Because the game uses social battle regions, visibility is a core retention pillar.

Players should be able to see:
- nearby squads;
- stand statistics;
- prestige;
- rare fighters;
- unusual effects.

### Important clarification
The social shell should be treated as:
- **ambient comparison**
- **aspiration**
- **visible progress**
- **conversation context**

It should not be overestimated as automatic deep social gameplay by itself.

---

## 10. Stand / Spot Statistics

Each battle stand / combat spot may show visible statistics.

### Strong candidate metrics
- Peak DPS
- Highest Crit
- Kill Streak
- Region Kills
- Boss Damage later

### Design rule
The stand should not become an Excel board.

The ideal display is:
- 1 main stat
- 1–2 secondary stats
- maybe title/prestige marker

### Recommended baseline
- Main: Peak DPS or Highest Crit
- Secondary: Kill Streak
- Secondary: Region Kills

---

## 11. Prestige Display

The player's stand should gradually become a visible identity object.

### Potential prestige layers
- stand skin;
- board style;
- aura;
- banner;
- title;
- squad display flair;
- visible best-fighter display later.

### Role
Prestige supports:
- retention;
- comparison;
- spending motivation;
- social differentiation.

---

## 12. Visible Progress Bars

Good bars include:
- fighter level;
- region progression;
- Dungeon progress track;
- Raid progress track;
- Bestiary progress;
- stand milestone progress later if needed.

### Important rule
A progress bar should lead to something visible enough to matter.

---

## 13. Guided Reveal

The game now explicitly uses a **guided reveal** philosophy.

A good deeper system should not sit hidden forever waiting for the player to study menus.

The game should reveal it when:
- the player feels friction;
- the next useful system can solve that friction;
- the payoff can be felt quickly.

### Core rule
A new system should appear as:
- a solution;
- a power spike;
- a visible next step.

Not as:
- abstract complexity;
- menu homework;
- a future promise with no immediate feel.

---

## 14. Guidance Stack

The baseline guidance stack is:

1. **Reactive Prompts**
2. **Regional Guidance Chain**
3. **Repeatable Tasks / Boards**
4. **Recommended** as the main next-action screen

### Reactive prompts
Used when:
- the player has enough resources to craft;
- a new recipe matters;
- a node unlocks;
- the player first fails;
- TTK gets meaningfully worse.

### Regional guidance chain
A short region-specific step chain that teaches:
- what this region farms;
- what useful item this region introduces;
- how that item helps;
- what node the player should try next.

### Repeatable tasks / boards
These provide:
- direction;
- bonus value;
- medium-length goals.

They should not replace free play.

---

## 15. Strong Early Experience

Early game should not be slow and “honest.”
It should be juicy.

### Early game should include
- fast first fights;
- quick squad completion;
- early new fighter reveals;
- at least one clearly better reward;
- fast visible power spikes;
- fast access to the real region loop;
- one clear guided next step when friction first appears.

---

## 16. Recurring Retention Systems

The game should also include:
- online time rewards;
- daily login rewards;
- boards/tasks;
- visible progress;
- clear next-action reminders.

These systems help:
- stabilize session length;
- create comeback habits;
- create “just a bit longer” pressure.

They should support the core loop, not replace it.

---

## 17. Design Intent Summary

The project should retain not because the player studies complexity,
but because the game repeatedly delivers:

- action;
- visible growth;
- surprise;
- comparison;
- and a clear next useful move.

That is the product-facing difference between:
- a merely complex system;
- and a sticky progression game.
